# Monolith Phase One Rewrite Brief

This brief turns the copy system into an actionable first rewrite pass.

The goal is not to rewrite every page at once.

The goal is to fix the strategic center of the site first, so all later rewrites inherit the same language system.

## 1. Phase One Objective

Bring the copy up to the level of the design.

Specifically:

- clarify what Monolith is within the first screen
- replace generic event language with ownable narrative
- make the brand feel culturally coherent, not page-by-page improvised
- strengthen ticket confidence and event clarity
- build a repeatable copy system for later pages

## 2. Phase One Deliverables

- Narrative foundation for Monolith
- Rewritten above-the-fold strategy for core pages
- New headline and subhead system
- CTA library for ticket, exploration, partner, and retention flows
- Proof inventory requirements for each page
- Microcopy direction for forms, FAQs, logistics, and conversion surfaces

## 3. Non-Goals

- Rewriting every page in one sprint
- Writing manifestos with no conversion function
- Making the site sound more poetic at the expense of clarity
- Replacing operations copy with mood language

## 4. Core Narrative Decisions

These decisions should stay consistent across every rewrite.

### Brand House Constraint

Monolith should be treated as a brand house with four core expressions:

- The Monolith Project
- Chasing Sun(Sets)
- Chasing Sun(Sets) Radio Show
- Untold Story

This means:

- the umbrella brand explains the overall world
- each series needs a distinct emotional temperature
- the radio show acts as a taste and retention surface, not a side feature
- every page should clarify whether it is speaking for the umbrella brand or a series inside it

### Decision 1

Monolith is not just an event promoter.

It is a cultural project built through events, series, archive, and recurring identity.

### Decision 2

The site should sell the room, not just the lineup.

Artists matter, but the brand difference lives in atmosphere, curation, and return-worthy rooms.

### Decision 3

Chicago is part of the credibility.

The city should appear as context, not as decoration.

### Decision 4

The brand should sound music-first and taste-led, not status-led.

### Decision 5

Operational clarity is part of the premium experience.

Users should never have to guess what to do next or what to expect.

## 5. Core Voice Decisions

- Write like a precise host, not a loud promoter.
- Use short declarative lines above the fold.
- Use sensory detail only when it clarifies the room.
- Use proof to support ambition.
- Cut any line a competitor could reuse unchanged.

## 6. Proof Priorities

Before major rewrites, gather these inputs.

- Artist and lineup credibility
- Venue credibility and distinctiveness
- Recurring series structure
- Archive / past night documentation
- Partner and media credibility
- Chicago-rooted scene relevance
- Ticket and hospitality clarity

## 8. Page Briefs

## Home

- Route: `/`
- Main job: Define Monolith fast and route people to the right next action
- User state: Curious, skeptical, visually interested, not yet fully oriented
- Primary audience: First-time guest with taste
- Secondary audience: Returning guest
- Primary CTA: Get Tickets or View Schedule
- Secondary CTA: Enter The Collective

### Home Messaging Goal

The homepage should answer:

- What is Monolith?
- Why should I care?
- What kind of nights does it create?
- Where should I go next?

### Recommended Home Structure

1. Hero:
   - define the project in one ownable line
   - surface the current live or next event
   - give one action CTA and one exploration CTA
2. Brand clarity block:
   - explain Monolith in plain language
3. Ecosystem block:
   - show events, series, archive, and people
4. Proof block:
   - artists, room, archive, Chicago relevance
5. Conversion block:
   - schedule, tickets, inner circle

### Home Hero Draft Direction

- H1 should define the world and the standard
- Subhead should explain who it is for and why it matters
- CTA 1 should be direct
- CTA 2 should reduce friction for users not ready to buy

## Tickets

- Route: `/tickets`
- Main job: Convert intent into action
- User state: Interested, comparing, needs confidence
- Primary audience: Ready-to-buy guest
- Secondary audience: Event-curious guest
- Primary CTA: Buy Tickets
- Secondary CTA: View Event Details

### Tickets Messaging Goal

The tickets page should feel like premium hospitality plus clear conversion.

It should answer:

- What event am I buying into?
- Why is this worth it?
- What am I getting?
- What do I need to know before purchase?

### Tickets Copy Priorities

- Lead with event and room quality
- Make ticket states clear
- Reduce uncertainty
- Keep logistics visible
- Remove unnecessary filler

## About

- Route: `/about`
- Main job: Explain Monolith as a brand
- User state: Curious but unconvinced
- Primary audience: Curious guest, press, partner
- Secondary audience: Returning guest
- Primary CTA: Explore Schedule
- Secondary CTA: Contact / Read More

### About Messaging Goal

The page should make Monolith legible as:

- a cultural project
- a curation standard
- a recurring room-building practice

### About Copy Priorities

- Explain why Monolith exists
- Define what it values
- Show how events, series, and archive connect
- Avoid abstract brand language without proof

## Chasing Sun(Sets)

- Route: `/chasing-sunsets`
- Main job: Sell the identity of the series
- User state: Curious about the series world
- Primary audience: Style- and atmosphere-led guest
- Secondary audience: Returning series follower
- Primary CTA: Explore Next Chapter / Get Tickets
- Secondary CTA: Join Inner Circle

### Chasing Sun(Sets) Narrative

This series should feel:

- open-air
- warm
- communal
- rhythmic
- cinematic at golden hour

### Chasing Sun(Sets) Copy Priorities

- Define the identity of the series
- Show what makes it different from the rest of Monolith
- Use atmosphere and timing as core descriptors
- Keep the invitation warm, not vague

## Chasing Sun(Sets) Radio Show

- Route: `/radio`
- Main job: turn listening into proof of taste and a recurring touchpoint between events
- User state: curious, already interested, or looking for a lower-friction way into the world
- Primary audience: returning guests and music-first first-timers
- Secondary audience: artists, press, and scene-adjacent listeners
- Primary CTA: Listen / Open Episode
- Secondary CTA: Explore Series / View Event World

### Radio Narrative

The radio show should feel:

- curated
- repeatable
- transportive
- tied to the Chasing Sun(Sets) world
- like an entry point into taste, not just a content shelf

### Radio Copy Priorities

- frame the show as a signal and proof of taste, not an archive dump
- explain what listening gives the audience
- make episodes feel selected, not merely posted
- connect the listening surface back to the wider Monolith world

## Untold Story

- Route: `/story`
- Main job: Sell the closer, deeper, more late-night side of the brand
- User state: Curious about identity, edge, and room character
- Primary audience: Late-night guest with stronger music intent
- Secondary audience: Artist / scene-aware guest
- Primary CTA: Explore Next Chapter / Get Tickets
- Secondary CTA: Read The Story

### Untold Story Narrative

This series should feel:

- more intimate
- more nocturnal
- more immersive in the true sense
- more tension-driven
- more music- and room-led than lifestyle-led

### Untold Story Copy Priorities

- Explain the emotional and musical identity of the series
- Make the room feel deliberate
- Use sharper language than the main brand
- Avoid going so abstract that the user loses the offer

## 9. Headline Strategy

### What Headlines Should Do

- define
- differentiate
- orient
- invite action

### What Headlines Should Not Do

- sound like poster taglines only
- rely on adjectives without meaning
- be mysterious at the expense of clarity

### Recommended Headline Formula

`[World / standard] + [who it is for or what it delivers]`

Examples of direction, not final copy:

- Chicago nights built with taste, rhythm, and a room worth returning to.
- A music-first event world with memory, atmosphere, and structure.
- Recurring rooms for people who care what the night feels like.

## 10. CTA Strategy

Use a tight CTA system.

### Primary

- Get Tickets
- View Schedule
- Explore The Series
- Request Access
- Inquire

### Secondary

- Read The Story
- Open Archive
- Listen To Radio
- Join Inner Circle

## 11. Missing Inputs To Gather

These would strengthen the rewrite immediately:

- final event calendar priorities
- proof of recurring attendance / demand
- partner / venue proof
- artist quotes or press lines if available
- archive selections that support each series identity
- real language from guests or collaborators worth adapting

## 12. Success Criteria

Phase one is successful if:

- a first-time user can define Monolith in one sentence after landing
- core pages sound like one brand, not several moods
- the event and series pages feel distinct from each other
- ticket and logistics copy build trust instead of friction
- the site feels more ownable in language, not just in design
