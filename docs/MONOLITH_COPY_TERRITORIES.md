# Monolith Copy Territories

This document defines the distinct copy territory for each expression in the Monolith brand house.

Use it before writing any page, headline, CTA, event description, or campaign asset.

If a line could belong to all four expressions equally, it is probably too generic.

## 1. The Monolith Project

### Role

The umbrella brand.

### Job

Define the world, the standard, and the relationship between the series, the radio signal, and the archive.

### Emotional Promise

There is a larger world here, and it is built with intention.

### What It Should Own

- the overall worldview
- Chicago-rooted cultural gravity
- curation standard
- recurring rooms and return behavior
- structure, continuity, and archive logic

### Tone

- assured
- architectural
- music-first
- culturally literate
- clear enough to guide action

### Lexicon To Favor

- world
- standard
- room
- chapter
- recurring
- archive
- curation
- Chicago-rooted
- atmosphere
- return
- collective
- togetherness

### Lexicon To Avoid

- party brand
- luxury destination
- unforgettable experience
- exclusive vibes
- scene-leading unless proven directly

### H1 Territory

- Chicago music nights built with taste, atmosphere, and rooms worth returning to.
- A Chicago-rooted music world built through recurring rooms and a record around them.
- Four connected expressions. One shared standard.

### CTA Territory

- View Schedule
- Enter The Collective
- Open Insights
- Get Tickets

### What It Should Never Sound Like

- a generic event promoter
- a lifestyle brand with no operational clarity
- a pseudo-spiritual manifesto with no proof

## 2. Chasing Sun(Sets)

### Role

The warmer, open-air live expression.

### Job

Carry the social, open, golden-hour side of Monolith.

### Emotional Promise

An open-air room that moves with the light and gives people a way into the city’s softer, warmer rhythm.

### What It Should Own

- golden hour
- warmth
- openness
- social energy
- dusk transitions
- outdoor pacing

### Tone

- warm
- inviting
- rhythmic
- breathable
- cinematic without being precious

### Lexicon To Favor

- golden hour
- open air
- dusk
- warmth
- slow build
- rooftop
- sunlight
- city heat
- gathering
- rhythmic

### Lexicon To Avoid

- beach-club cliches
- generic sunset spirituality
- influencer-travel language
- empty “vibes” copy

### H1 Territory

- Open-air chapters built for the shift from daylight into dusk.
- Golden-hour rooms shaped by rhythm, warmth, and the people inside them.
- A warmer Monolith expression built for rooftops, light, and movement.

### CTA Territory

- Explore Sun(Sets)
- View Next Chapter
- Join Inner Circle
- Listen To The Radio Show

### Proof Territory

- open-air venues
- rooftop and outdoor settings
- recurring seasonal logic
- music that moves from day into night

## 3. Chasing Sun(Sets) Radio Show

### Role

The listening and editorial extension of the Sun(Sets) world.

### Job

Keep the taste, pacing, and signal alive between live chapters.

### Emotional Promise

You can step into the world before the night and stay with it after.

### What It Should Own

- listening signal
- artist sessions
- mixes
- conversations
- signal between nights
- lower-friction entry into the brand

### Tone

- curated
- transportive
- intimate
- editorial
- less sales-driven than the event pages

### Lexicon To Favor

- signal
- listening signal
- session
- transmission
- pacing
- mix
- selection
- listen back
- between nights

### Lexicon To Avoid

- content hub
- podcast network
- blog language
- generic “tap in” phrasing

### H1 Territory

- A listening signal from the Sun(Sets) world.
- Mixes, artist sessions, and signal between chapters.
- Hear the taste behind the room before you arrive.

### CTA Territory

- Listen Now
- Open Episode
- Hear The Signal
- Explore Sun(Sets)

### Proof Territory

- recurring episodes
- artist voices
- curated selection logic
- press or listener recognition if available

## 4. Untold Story

### Role

The closer, more intimate, late-night live expression.

### Job

Carry the deeper, closer, more tension-led side of Monolith.

### Emotional Promise

The room narrows, the sound gets deeper, and the night feels more intentional as it goes on.

### What It Should Own

- intimacy
- tension
- late-night pacing
- closeness between artist and crowd
- focused energy
- deeper musical framing

### Tone

- sharper
- closer
- more intimate
- more focused
- more deliberate, but still grounded

### Lexicon To Favor

- late night
- closer room
- tension
- low light
- deeper
- intimate
- focused
- late-hour
- inside the room

### Lexicon To Avoid

- gothic theater language
- empty mystery
- pseudo-secret-society copy
- “immersive” without real explanation

### H1 Territory

- The room changes shape as the night goes on.
- A closer room with deeper sound and sharper tension.
- Intimate nights built for the later hours, when the city gets quieter and the room gets louder.

### CTA Territory

- Enter Untold Story
- View Next Chapter
- Get Tickets
- Read The Story

### Proof Territory

- intimate venue size
- closer DJ / crowd relationship
- deeper lineups
- sold-out rooms if true
- distinct production or layout choices if true

## 5. Shared House Rules

All four expressions should share these truths:

- music-first
- room-led
- Chicago-rooted
- curated rather than generic
- clear enough to act on

## 6. Shared Words To Use Carefully

These words are allowed, but only with discipline:

- community
- immersive
- collective
- togetherness
- cultural
- elevated
- world

They become weak when used without proof.

## 7. Shared Banned Phrases

- unforgettable night
- one-of-a-kind experience
- luxury nightlife destination
- elevated vibes
- epic lineup
- can’t-miss event
- where music meets magic
- vibrant community
- exclusive atmosphere

## 8. Litmus Test

Before approving any line, ask:

- Is this clearly Monolith, Sun(Sets), Radio Show, or Untold Story?
- Could a competitor say this unchanged?
- Does the line describe a room, a rhythm, or a real point of proof?
- Does it sound like a host with taste or a promoter with a megaphone?
